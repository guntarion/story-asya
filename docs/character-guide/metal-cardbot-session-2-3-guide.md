# Metal Cardbot Season 2 & 3 - Character Guide

> Reference guide for writing stories based on Metal Cardbot Season 2 (Metal Cardbot S) and Season 3 (Metal Cardbot W) characters.
> Source: [Metal Cardbot Wiki (Fandom)](https://metal-cardbot.fandom.com/wiki/)

---

## Season 2 Overview - Metal Cardbot S

Season 2 (Metal Cardbot S) expands the timeline dramatically, revealing that Cardbots visited Earth **4,000 years ago**. The colony ship **Speranza** crash-landed on ancient Earth, and its crew formed bonds with a human village called **Porcaca Village**, especially with a human named **Mukara**. When resources ran low, Mukara sealed the Speranza Crew using a Metal Breath given to him by Gigantrex and hid their Metal Cards as stone tablets in the **Porcaca Ruins temple**.

In the present day, the stone tablets have been stolen and scattered. Jun Grant, now a seasoned partner, must work alongside a new human ally -- **Peruru**, a descendant of Mukara -- to awaken and reunite the Speranza Crew while confronting new threats.

### Key Season 2 Concepts
- **Speranza**: Ancient Cardbot colony ship that crashed on Earth 4,000 years ago. Contains 2 billion sealed Cardbots.
- **Porcaca Village / Ruins**: Ancient human settlement that befriended the Speranza Crew. The ruins held their sealed Metal Cards.
- **Mukara**: Ancient human who bonded with the Speranza Crew. Ancestor of Peruru. Received a Metal Breath from Gigantrex.
- **Machinasium Spear**: A relic from Mukara capable of healing Cardbot mental corruption.
- **Organizer**: A portal device stored inside certain Cardbots' chests (Gigantrex, Red Blitz). Can open portals of varying sizes.
- **Hidden Cardbots**: A faction of Cardbots (Flash Vector, Spark Bit, Sky Gallop) operating independently, later manipulated by Flame Nova.
- **Cosmic Stones**: Prism-based rocks that amplify a Cardbot's power at the cost of rendering them berserk. Stolen by Flame Nova from Deus Machina's sanctuary.
- **Blue Cop Trinity**: Blue Cop's Season 2 gestalt upgrade, combining his base body with a SWAT truck and police bus. Equal in height to Heavy Iron, Gigantrex, Sky Gallop, and Flame Nova.

## Season 2 Human Characters

### Peruru (페루루) - The Heir of Porcaca

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 페루루 (Pelulu) |
| **Age** | 12-13 |
| **Gender** | Female |
| **Hair** | Red |
| **Eyes** | Red |
| **Family** | Mukara (ancestor), Maruru (mother) |
| **Metal Breath** | Mukara's Metal Breath |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | Eunae Kang |

**Role**: Secondary protagonist of Season 2. Wielder of Mukara's Metal Breath and caretaker of the Porcaca Ruins.

**Background**: Peruru was raised by her mother Maruru in Porcaca Village, where she developed tremendous athletic ability. As caretaker of the Porcaca Ruins temple, she guarded the four stone tablets containing the sealed Speranza Crew -- until they were all stolen. Armed with Mukara's Metal Breath and a Machinasium spear, she traveled to Mowtown to recover the stolen Cardbots. When she discovered Red Blitz had stolen Jun's Metal Breath, she offered to replace it with Mukara's if Jun could prove himself worthy. They became allies, and Peruru eventually gained the ability to use the Metal Breath herself.

**Personality**: Energetic and athletic, with a strong sense of duty inherited from her ancestral connection to the Cardbots. She wears pointed red eye makeup as a traditional Porcaca Village style. Initially guarded but becomes a close ally to Jun and the team.

**Appearance**: Red hair tied back in two braids (redone into a bun when sleeping). Primary outfit is a short-sleeved athleticwear jumpsuit with a traditionally patterned scarf tied around the waist, compression sleeves, and sandals. When traveling, she covers herself with a maroon poncho, baseball hat, and face mask. Keeps the Machinasium spear in a carrying case with a leather strap. Later borrows Jun's clothes including a varsity jacket and black jeans.

**Key Trait**: Her Machinasium spear can heal Cardbot mental corruption -- she thrust it into Gigantrex's head wound to restore his sanity.

**Cardbot Collection**: Gigantrex, Red Blitz, Cielo, Musclehyde, Deep Bite, Sky Gallop, Spark Bit, Flash Vector

## Season 2 Cardbots - The Speranza Crew

The Speranza Crew was the elite team that piloted the colony ship Speranza from the dying Planet Machina. They crashed on Earth 4,000 years ago and were sealed in stone tablets by their human ally Mukara. Each crew member was painted with a symbol representing their bond with Mukara.

### 1. Gigantrex (기간트렉스) - The Fallen Captain

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 기간트렉스 (Giganteulegseu) |
| **Affiliation** | Speranza Crew (Captain) / Porcaca Village |
| **Vehicle Mode** | Tractor Truck + Tanker Trailer |
| **Metal Card** | Rex Arm |
| **Color** | Red |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | An Jang-hyuk (안장혁) |

**Role**: Captain of the Speranza. The highest-ranking Cardbot of Season 2, entrusted by Deus Machina to safeguard 2 billion sealed Cardbots aboard the Speranza and find a new homeworld.

**Background**: On Planet Machina, Gigantrex was the captain of the spaceship Speranza and prepared it for a journey to establish a new civilization. He invited his trusted associates Red Blitz, Cielo, and Musclehyde as his bridge crew. Deus Machina secretly warned him of Machina's impending destruction. After the Speranza crashed on Earth due to Red Blitz's misguided attempt to save Machina, the crew befriended the people of Porcaca Village, especially Mukara, to whom Gigantrex gifted his Metal Breath and a Machinasium spear. When resources ran low, Mukara sealed them in stone tablets in the Porcaca Ruins temple. In the present day, Gigantrex's stone tablet ended up on a cargo ship that was attacked by a crazed Deep Bite. When unsealed, Deep Bite bit into Gigantrex's head, infecting him with the Beast Virus and causing erratic, aggressive behavior. He was eventually restored when Peruru thrust her Machinasium spear into his head wound.

**Personality**: Commanding and authoritative as a captain should be. A figure of great respect and responsibility who carries the burden of 2 billion sealed lives. Even while corrupted by the head wound, glimpses of his noble nature showed through.

**Appearance**: A red Cardbot with a colossal body equal in height to Heavy Iron. Black/silver/yellow/white coloration throughout. His head resembles the Speranza in shape with a fang-style symbol and booster-based flap on each side. Light-blue eyes. Broad red shoulders with yellow/white base. Black arms with silver/yellow/red detailing. Metal panel on each forearm. Matching yellow and black symbols on the chest and forehead. Tires, mudflaps, and suspension on his back can convert into thrusters for speed. Bears a symbol painted by Mukara on his right shoulder representing their bond. He sustained a head wound from Deep Bite that generated sparks and electricity (repaired by Peruru), and a diagonal chest slash from Blue Cop Trinity (repaired after the battle for the Speranza).

**Quote**: *"I am Gigantrex, Captain of the Speranza."*

**Arsenal & Techniques**:
- **Rex Arm (렉스 암)**: A large Tyrannosaurus-rex head gauntlet mounted on Gigantrex's left arm. The first Metal Card weapon in the series as large as its user's body.
- **Death Grip (데스 그립)**: A giant pincer grip using the Rex Arm's fangs to crush targets. Powerful enough to destroy Blue Cop S's Justice Gauntlet and Musclehyde's Crane Site, and deal severe damage to Cardbots.
- **Rex Inferno (렉스 인페르노)**: Wide-area flame attack activated by slamming the Rex Arm filled with flame energy into the ground, burning the surrounding area. A variant fires a flame beam from the Rex Arm jaws.
- **Rex Blade (렉스 블레이드)**: A wrist blade that unfolds from the metal panel on Gigantrex's arms.
- **Vulcan**: Like Heavy Iron, Gigantrex can fire energy bullets from exhaust ports on both shoulders.
- **Organizer (오거나이저)**: A portal device stored inside Gigantrex's chest. Gigantrex is adept at using it in combat, though overuse caused it to malfunction and harm him until the Cardbots destroyed it.

**Relationships**: Father figure to Red Blitz. Trusted comrades with Cielo and Musclehyde. Befriended Mukara 4,000 years ago. Allies with Peruru and Jun Grant. Deep Bite bit his head, causing his corruption (Deep Bite later joined the Speranza crew to atone). In Metal Cardbot W, it was revealed Gigantrex is a Wild Cardbot carrier from being bitten by Deep Bite.

**Story Notes**: Gigantrex is the first Metal Cardbot in the series to possess a Weapon Card as large as the user's body. His Rex Blade deployment differs between the show (pocket knife fold-out) and the toy set (both gauntlet sides slide open). His scale matches Heavy Iron's, making him one of the largest Cardbots in the franchise.

### 2. Red Blitz (레드블리츠) - The Red Flash

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 레드블리츠 (Ledeu Beullicheu) |
| **Affiliation** | Speranza Crew / Edo Motors / Porcaca Village |
| **Vehicle Mode** | Muscle Car (Toyota 86-based) |
| **Metal Card** | Boost Gambiera |
| **Color** | Red |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | Jeon Tae-yeol (전태열) |

**Role**: Ship engineer of the Speranza. The youngest member of the Speranza Crew.

**Background**: On Planet Machina, Red Blitz was a troublemaker who tried to fight the entire Speranza crew single-handedly before joining them. He served as the ship's engineer. After escaping Machina, Red Blitz made a misguided attempt to save the planet that caused the Speranza to crash on Earth. After befriending Porcaca Village and Mukara, he was sealed in a stone tablet. In the present day, his tablet was sold to Crest by a black market dealer. After being unsealed, Red Blitz stole Jun's Metal Breath, leading to Peruru offering Mukara's as a replacement. Unlike other sealed Cardbots who mellow out quickly, Red Blitz remained uncooperative and unfriendly toward Jun for weeks, only gradually accepting him.

**Personality**: Aggressive, impulsive, and impatient. The most fight-happy member of the crew, resiliently stubborn against efforts to correct him. The only individual who can command him without rebuff is Gigantrex, due to his status as Red Blitz's mentor and father figure. Hyper-fixated on his home and Gigantrex, often behaving irrationally and complicating things unnecessarily. Rarely thinks things through and starts fights over simple misunderstandings. Over time, he becomes more reliable and capable of tempering his impulses.

**Appearance**: A red Cardbot with a sleek body based on the Muay Thai fighting style. Sleek head with a golden curving crest along the outer portion and a golden three-grooved symbol with a golden flap on each side. Green eyes. Black torso, shoulders, arms, and upper legs. Red plate with large curving crest and muscle car engine on the chest. Red hip with golden buckle. Red lower legs. Red armor with wheels on the shoulders and legs. Bears a Mukara bond symbol on his left shoulder.

**Quote**: *"I am the Red Flash, Red Blitz!"*

**Arsenal & Techniques**:
- **Boost Gambiera (부스트 감비에라)**: A pair of taloned feet extensions added to Red Blitz's legs to enhance his kicking power.
- **Scarlet Hurricane (스칼렛 허리케인)**: Main ultimate. Creates a whirlwind of red energy and kicks it toward the opponent, knocking them back.
- **Scarlet Typhoon (스칼렛 타이푼)**: Creates a red energy wheel and kicks it toward the opponent.
- **Crimson Shoot (크림슨 슛)**: Generates and kicks a red sphere at the opponent.
- **Crimson Kick (크림슨 킥)**: Generates crimson flames around the Boost Gambiera and kicks the opponent.
- **Mega Crimson Kick (메가 크림슨 킥)**: Enhanced Crimson Kick. Increases the flame output around the Boost Gambiera and executes a kick wrapped in blue flames.
- **Organizer (오거나이저)**: A portal device stored inside Red Blitz's chest. Can open and alter portal size by turning a dial. Jun broke the device after fidgeting with it in Episode 2.

**Relationships**: Views Gigantrex as a father figure and mentor. Fellow Speranza crew with Cielo and Musclehyde. Befriended Mukara and Porcaca Village. Initially hostile to Jun Grant but gradually accepted him. Eventually befriended by Kent.

**Story Notes**: Red Blitz shares personality similarities with Flame Nova (stubborn, aggressive, fight-happy) but the key difference is that Red Blitz has genuinely benevolent intentions and can be convinced to set aside his annoyances when needed, whereas Flame Nova is actively malicious. He is responsible for crashing the Speranza due to his misguided attempt to save Machina.

### 3. Cielo (시에로) - He Who Conquers the Sky

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 시에로 (Sielo) |
| **Affiliation** | Speranza Crew / Edo Motors / Porcaca Village |
| **Vehicle Mode** | Fighter Twinjet (Yak-130-based) |
| **Metal Card** | Nitro Express |
| **Color** | Cerulean |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | Jo Hyun-jung (조현정) -- female VA |

**Role**: Navigator of the Speranza. Aerial combat specialist.

**Background**: On Planet Machina, Cielo was a member of Gigantrex's crew and served as the ship's navigator. After the Speranza crash-landed on Earth, Cielo and the crew befriended Porcaca Village, especially Mukara. He was sealed in a stone tablet that ended up on display in the Mowtown Museum. His Mukara bond symbol was painted on the right side of his cap crest; it faded over time, leading Jun Grant to spray-paint it back on.

**Personality**: Often impulsive, diving into situations based on intuition. Makes a conscious effort to get along with humans, though it can be a struggle depending on the person. Slow to warm up, but once he opens up, he is clear about his affection and occasionally teases those younger than him. Incredibly loyal and always willing to use his strength and skill to defend allies.

**Appearance**: A cerulean Cardbot with an air force combat uniform motif. Pilot helmet/hood/cap-based head. Green eyes. White upper arms and upper legs. Orange-colored rims throughout the upper body. Silver wrists. Black hands. Cyan neon lights in the upper body. Wings and boosters on the shoulders that retain their flight features. Crosshair marks on the chest and a green light variant on each side of the head. Black feet with cerulean base.

**Quote**: *"He who conquers the sky! It is I, Cielo!"*

**Arsenal & Techniques**:
- **Nitro Express (니트로 익스프레스)**: A pair of machine guns firmly fixed through the joints on Cielo's forearms.
- **Sky Jet Shot (스카이 제트 샷)**: Fires arrow-shaped energy bullets from the Nitro Express. Capable of penetrating concrete in seconds.
- **Homing Magnum (호밍 매그넘)**: Main ultimate. Fires energy bullets with guided missile properties. Unlike Sky Jet Shot's continuous fire, fires in small amounts to avoid detection by the opponent.
- **Missile Launcher**: Cielo can reconfigure both arms into missile launchers to fire energy beams.

**Relationships**: Loyal member of Gigantrex's Speranza Crew alongside Musclehyde and Red Blitz. Befriended Mukara. Allies with Jun Grant, Peruru, and the Edo Motors group.

**Story Notes**: Cielo is the first Cardbot to have a female voice actor. Despite this, a toy designer confirmed Cielo is not a female character. The toy set has light-blue eyes while the animation model changed them to green.

### 4. Musclehyde (머슬하이드) - The Steel Arm

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 머슬하이드 (Meoseulhaideu) |
| **Affiliation** | Speranza Crew / Edo Motors / Porcaca Village |
| **Vehicle Mode** | All-Terrain Crane (Kobelco KMG5220-based) |
| **Metal Card** | Variable Arms / Crane Bhuh |
| **Color** | Caribbean-Green |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | Kwon Chang-wook (권창욱) |

**Role**: Pilot of the Speranza. Former reporter for the Cardian Post.

**Background**: On Planet Machina, Musclehyde was a reporter for the Cardian Post before Gigantrex asked him to join the Speranza crew as its pilot. After the crash on Earth and bonding with Porcaca Village, he was sealed in a stone tablet that ended up in a cargo plane in transit between unknown locations. Bears a Mukara bond symbol on the right side of his chest.

**Personality**: As a former reporter, Musclehyde is intelligent and inquisitive, asking plenty of questions in unfamiliar situations. Usually composed and courteous, especially to those he feels loyal to, but feels strongly and does not hesitate to use force when upset. Has a dual personality mechanic: he can shift his horns to focus aggression. When horns are down, he is in his docile persona; when horns are upright, the forehead guard comes down, the "M" glows red, and his eyes become fierce to show his violent personality. (This is purely symbolic and has no tangible combat effect.)

**Appearance**: A Caribbean-green/white large Cardbot with a bull motif. Bull-based head with an axe symbol on each side. Guard with an "M"-based symbol on the forehead. Yellow eyes. Yellow axe-shaped horns that change position based on personality state. Black shoulders and upper arms. Golden-yellow circles on the elbows. Two-barreled armor plating on the shoulders and forearms. Caribbean-green/white torso with markings. Bull horn-designed buckles on the white hip. Two-digit black feet with a Caribbean-green base. Back unit stores the Variable Arms.

**Quote**: *"I'm Musclehyde, the Steel Arm. Would you be okay with an interview?"*

**Arsenal & Techniques**:
- **Variable Arms (베리어블 암)**: A pair of additional arms attached to Musclehyde's back unit, granting him a total of four arms.
- **Crane Bhuh (크레인 부지)**: Musclehyde's axe made from the axe blade on the Variable Arms in storage and his mace. (The word "Bhuh" is based on the Sanskrit word "bhuh" meaning "earth" or "the lower planetary systems".)
- **Mace**: Main weapon made from his vehicle form's crane boom. When not in use, it hangs on his back in shortened form. The hook on the other side remains usable, allowing the crane boom to double as a fishing rod.
- **Brachio Slash (브라키오 슬래쉬)**: Main ultimate. Wraps green energy around the Crane Bhuh and strikes the opponent with a shockwave powerful enough to fracture the ground.
- **Bronto Strike (브론토 스트라이크)**: Slams the energized Crane Bhuh down, creating a powerful crack in the ground.
- **Dreadnought Storm (드레드노트 스톰)**: Rotates the Crane Bhuh so fast it leaves a green afterimage before attacking.

**Relationships**: Loyal member of Gigantrex's Speranza Crew alongside Cielo and Red Blitz. Befriended Mukara. Allies with Jun Grant, Peruru, and the Edo Motors group.

**Story Notes**: The names of Musclehyde's attacks are based on three sauropod dinosaur species: Brachiosaurus, Brontosaurus, and Dreadnoughtus. The toy set shows shoulder armor and gauntlets that convert into vulcan firearms, but they were never used in the show. Despite having a Korean name translation that lists "기간트렉스" (Gigantrex), the actual Korean for Musclehyde is 머슬하이드.

## Season 2 New Cardbots

These Cardbots are not part of the Speranza Crew but arrived on Earth independently via escape pods after Machina's destruction.

### 5. Glober (글로버) - The World Explorer

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 글로버 (Geullobeo) |
| **Affiliation** | Edo Motors |
| **Vehicle Mode** | 3-door Off-Road SUV (Mitsubishi Pajero-based) |
| **Metal Card** | Shadow Whip |
| **Color** | Munsell-Yellow |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | Sang-Hyeon Eom (엄상현) |

**Role**: An adventurer and explorer who fell in love with Earth's natural beauty. Friends with Rock Crush since their days on Planet Machina.

**Background**: Glober and Rock Crush were friends on Planet Machina. After its destruction, they drifted through space in individual escape pods until arriving on Earth. During landing, Glober's pod crashed due to an accident, separating them. After marveling at Earth's nature, Glober became enchanted with the planet and lives freely, traveling across six continents to take in the beautiful scenery. However, he learned of Rock Crush's traumatic incident that turned him into a reclusive loner. Glober confronted Gigantrex to save Wella but was severely injured in the process. He accepted Jun's request to be sealed in the Metal Breath to heal.

**Personality**: Adventurous, laid-back, and genuinely in love with Earth's natural world. An "Indiana Jones" archetype. Was against being sealed in the Metal Breath, preferring to roam free. Willing to risk his life for others.

**Appearance**: A munsell-yellow Cardbot with an explorer design. Web-orange head with a safari hat sporting a built-in flashlight. Compass symbol on each side. Green eyes. Large shoulders. Munsell-yellow torso with black base and web-orange center plate. Black upper arms. Munsell-yellow forearms with web-orange inner arm. Silver hands. Black markings on limbs (compass on right shoulder, stripes on left shoulder and legs). To blend in with humans, Glober uses a mannequin in his driver's seat named "Jones" -- dressed in outdoorsy clothes with a drawn-on face.

**Quote**: *"Oh, oh, sorry for the late introduction. I'm Glober. An adventurer who travels across six continents, you know?"*

**Arsenal & Techniques**:
- **Shadow Whip (쉐도우 윕)**: A gauntlet equipped with a firearm barrel and a metallic whip, attached to Glober's right arm.
- **Catcher Whip (캐쳐 윕)**: Uses the Shadow Whip to bind the opponent and immobilize them.
- **Thunder Whip (썬더 윕)**: Binds the opponent with the Shadow Whip, then emits a strong electric current to electrocute them.
- **Cutter Whip (커터 휩)**: Generates and unleashes one to multiple orange-yellow crescent energy slashes by cracking the Shadow Whip.

**Relationships**: Best friends with Rock Crush since Planet Machina. Also friends with Deep Bite (despite Deep Bite's fearsome reputation, Glober befriended him). Allies with Jun Grant and the entire Edo Motors team.

**Story Notes**: Glober's appearance, Metal Card Weapon, and adventurous nature are based on Disney's Indiana Jones franchise. His decoy mannequin is literally named "Jones." Shares a voice actor with Wild Guardy (Sang-Hyeon Eom).

### 6. Deep Bite (딥바이트) - The Outlaw of the Sea

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 딥바이트 (Dibbaiteu) |
| **Affiliation** | Edo Motors / Porcaca Village / Soleil (temporarily in S3) |
| **Vehicle Mode** | Submarine (shark-head design) |
| **Metal Card** | Deep Trident |
| **Color** | Ultramarine-Blue |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | Kim Myung-jun (김명준) |

**Role**: Aquatic combat specialist. Known as the "Outlaw of the Sea" on Planet Machina due to his bestial appearance, but is actually a gentle soul.

**Background**: On Planet Machina, Deep Bite was born a Wild Cardbot, which made him ostracized by the Metal Cardbots. Forced into isolation, he decided to act feral and attack anything on sight, earning his "Outlaw of the Sea" nickname. Despite his terrifying appearance, Glober befriended him and treated him with respect. After escaping Machina's destruction, Deep Bite arrived on Earth but sustained damage to his metal teeth, driving him crazy and making him attack anything underwater. He came across a freighter carrying Gigantrex's sealed stone tablet, sank it, and bit into Gigantrex's head when the captain was unsealed -- infecting Gigantrex with the Beast Virus and causing severe physical and mental damage. Deep Bite volunteered to be sealed by Jun after his appearance was accepted by Jun, Blue Cop, and Rock Crush. After the final battle for the Speranza, Deep Bite joined the Speranza crew to atone for biting Gigantrex and to search for the Organizer that fell into the lake.

**Personality**: Despite the fearsome reputation and bestial appearance, Deep Bite is a gentle soul underneath. His aggressive behavior stemmed from being ostracized as a Wild Cardbot and later from damaged teeth. Once accepted, he is loyal and eager to help.

**Appearance**: An ultramarine-blue Wild Cardbot with a shark/diver motif. Head with one shark dorsal fin on top and a fin on each side. Sea-green scuba mask connected to a snorkel fused with the jawline-style faceplate. Dorsal fin symbol on each side. Yellow distinctive eyes. White torso with black shark head on chest. Ultramarine-blue trapezoid shoulders with black vents. Forearms with fold-in fins and sea-green wrist rings. Black hands with claw-based fingers. Back unit comprised of shark motif parts (upper head, pectoral fins, small shark tail with two boosters). Vehicle form is a submarine with shark head, four pectoral fins, and the sail acting as the dorsal fin.

**Quote**: *"I am Deep Bite, the Outlaw of the Sea!"*

**Arsenal & Techniques**:
- **Deep Trident (딥 트라이던트)**: A large rifle with a trident blade serving as the muzzle. Carried in a holster on Deep Bite's right side as a firearm. In close combat, wielded as a spear.
- **Beam Trident (빔 트라이던트)**: Fires a high-pressure water stream from the Deep Trident's spear blade.
- **Trident Submarine (트라이던트 서브마린)**: Generates water at the Deep Trident's muzzle to create a waterspout and throws it at the enemy.
- **Trident Gatling (트라이던트 게틀링)**: The Deep Trident's blade rotates rapidly to fire water bolts like a machine gun.
- **Trident Cannon (트라이던트 캐논)**: Fires the Deep Trident's trident blade like a harpoon.
- **Abyss Cannon (어비스 캐논)**: A firearm with a bayonet wielded exclusively in Deep Bite's left hand. Accurate enough to hit drones from a distance. Stored on the left side of the waist when not in use.

**Relationships**: Friends with Glober (who befriended him despite his reputation). Allies with Jun Grant, Rock Crush, and the Edo Motors team. Bit Gigantrex's head (later atoned). In Season 3, was temporarily abducted by Soleil due to his Wild Cardbot carrier status, then returned to Jun.

**Story Notes**: Deep Bite is the only Metal Cardbot in the series to possess overt animal traits, later revealed as a Wild Cardbot. He is the second Cardbot after Black Hook to have a watercraft vehicle mode. While retroactively confirmed, Deep Bite is the first Wild Cardbot to appear in the series. The Beast Virus was the source of his tooth ailment.

### 7. Rock Crush (록크러쉬) - The Timid Miner

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Korean** | 록크러쉬 (Log Keuleoswi) |
| **Affiliation** | Edo Motors |
| **Vehicle Mode** | Drill Tank (tracked treads) |
| **Metal Card** | Freezer Chest |
| **Color** | Crimson / Indigo |
| **Voice Actor (KR)** | Lee Ji-hyun (이지현) -- female VA |

**Role**: Underground combat specialist. A young Cardbot traumatized into becoming a recluse after arriving on Earth.

**Background**: Rock Crush and Glober were friends on Planet Machina. After its destruction, they drifted through space in escape pods until arriving on Earth. During landing, Glober's pod crashed and they were separated. Rock Crush ended up in Mowtown and suffered from the overwhelming noise. Attempting to escape underground, he accidentally contacted a power transmission facility and was electrocuted. His already fragile personality was deeply affected, turning him into a reclusive loner who avoided everything and showed intense hostility toward anyone, friend or foe. After being sealed by Jun Grant, Rock Crush slowly began to open up.

**Personality**: Timid, fragile, and traumatized. Initially hostile to anyone who approached due to his PTSD-like condition from electrocution. Gradually becomes more open and brave after being sealed by Jun and receiving support from the team.

**Appearance**: An indigo Cardbot with a miner motif. Indigo mining helmet with flashlight on top. Crimson chin guard and crimson drill symbol on each side. Light-blue eyes. Green visor that allows him to see in infrared and soundwaves to compensate for poor eyesight. Crimson back, shoulders, and upper arms. Black forearms, silver hands. Indigo/crimson torso with golden-yellow center plate. Tread sets stored on back and legs. One half of his Megalo Drill placed on each arm as a gauntlet.

**Quote**: *"I won't run away anymore!"*

**Arsenal & Techniques**:
- **Freezer Chest (프리저 체스트)**: A drill-themed chest plate attached to Rock Crush's thorax.

## Season 3 Overview - Metal Cardbot W

Season 3 (Metal Cardbot W) introduces the **Wild Cardbots**, a divergent subspecies of Cardbots created by the **Beast Virus** -- a pathogen from Machina that rewrites a Cardbot's code, blending machinery with animal traits. This season shifts from ancient history to techno-organic horror, exploring the boundaries between mechanical and biological life.

### Key Season 3 Concepts
- **Beast Virus**: A pathogen from Planet Machina that infects Cardbots, giving them animalistic features (fangs, claws, fur patterns) and feral aggression. Wild Cardbots are carriers, and contact can spread the infection.
- **Wild Cardbots**: Cardbots born with or infected by the Beast Virus. They have distinctive hexagonal eyes with unique frames, animal motifs, and claw-based fingers. They were ostracized on Machina.
- **Wild Weapon Cards**: Translucent until scanned, reflecting the volatile nature of Wild Cardbot powers.
- **Blue Cop W**: Blue Cop's Season 3 evolution. After infection/adaptation, he gains a wolf motif and the Howl Gauntlet (wolf-head cannon). Transforms into a Chevrolet Corvette (C7) Police Car.
- **Heavy Iron W**: Heavy Iron's Season 3 form. He betrayed Jun Grant and the Metal Cardbots, forming an alliance with Solidhorn before being defeated. Deliberately infected himself with the Beast Virus using Solidhorn's dislodged Rhinohide, gaining a dragon motif.
- **Superior Unite (슈페리얼 유나이트)**: The process by which Jun and Soleil unite their Metal Breaths to form Grand Bluleon.
- **Trion Prison**: A prison on Planet Machina where dangerous Wild Cardbots like Solidhorn were incarcerated.

## Writing Tips for Season 2 & 3 Stories

### Season 2 Character Dynamics
- **Gigantrex**: The noble captain carrying the weight of 2 billion lives. His corruption and restoration arc provides dramatic tension. Use his authority and gravitas for leadership moments.
- **Red Blitz + Gigantrex**: Father-son dynamic. Red Blitz will do anything for his captain, even irrational things. Their reunion is emotionally charged.
- **Red Blitz vs. Jun**: Unlike other Cardbots who accept Jun quickly, Red Blitz's slow acceptance creates ongoing friction and comedic moments. He remains the most stubborn Cardbot.
- **Cielo**: The intuition-driven aerial specialist. His female voice actor gives him a distinct youthful quality. Good for banter and teasing younger characters.
- **Musclehyde**: The journalist-turned-warrior. His inquisitive nature means he asks lots of questions (good for exposition). His dual personality (docile vs. fierce) when shifting horns provides visual storytelling.
- **Glober + Rock Crush**: Glober is the confident adventurer; Rock Crush is the timid friend he's trying to coax out of his shell. Their friendship arc mirrors real-world social anxiety themes.
- **Deep Bite**: The gentle giant wrongly judged by his monstrous appearance. His arc is about acceptance -- being a Wild Cardbot among Metal Cardbots who feared him. Good for anti-bullying themes.
- **Blastrain**: Always speaks like a train conductor. Everything is "passengers," "stations," and "destinations." Great comic relief through his commitment to the conductor bit.
- **Peruru**: A strong female lead with ancient heritage. Her Machinasium spear and athletic abilities make her an active participant, not a damsel. Pair her with Jun for a dual-commander dynamic.

### Season 3 Character Dynamics
- **Soleil vs. Jun**: The dark mirror. Soleil forces Cardbot obedience while Jun earns friendship. This contrast is central to S3's themes. Soleil is not evil but misguided -- she believes the mission justifies the means.
- **Blaion + Soleil**: Like Blue Cop + Jun, but more serious and reserved. Blaion's warmth contrasts with Soleil's coldness.
- **Tigun**: The "strongest" who secretly wants peace. His character reveal (changed by a brave cat named Nightcrawler) is heartwarming. Good for stories about the difference between strength and bravery.
- **Night Griffon**: The narcissistic fighter whose predictability is his weakness. Fun villain-turned-ally moments when his ego is deflated.
- **Phantom Hornet**: The ninja assassin who just wants to be left alone. His Phantom-Legion (holographic clones) is great for action sequences.
- **Solidhorn**: The tragic chieftain whose entire clan is dead. His alliance with Heavy Iron (who also craves power) provides a "villain team-up" dynamic. His defeat leads to Grand Bluleon's creation.
- **Grand Bluleon**: The ultimate symbol of unity. Using this form in stories should feel earned and climactic -- it requires both Jun and Soleil cooperating, which is itself a narrative challenge given Soleil's rigid personality.

### Tone & Humor Sources (Season 2)
- Red Blitz picking fights over trivial misunderstandings
- Blastrain narrating everything like a train announcement
- Glober's mannequin "Jones" fooling humans (or not)
- Rock Crush's timid reactions contrasted with his powerful drill attacks
- Musclehyde interviewing enemies mid-battle (old reporter habits die hard)
- Deep Bite being the gentlest soul in the scariest body

### Tone & Humor Sources (Season 3)
- Tigun proclaiming himself strongest, then being soft around the cat Nightcrawler
- Night Griffon's narcissism getting him into traps
- Soleil's deadpan reactions to Jun's enthusiasm
- The contrast between Wild Cardbots' fierce animal appearances and their actual personalities
- Heavy Iron's betrayal and grumpy defiance continuing from S1
- Phantom Hornet's ninja stealth being used for mundane things

### Battle Structure (Season 2 & 3)
Season 2 and 3 battles follow a similar structure to S1 but with added complexity:
1. **Assessment**: Jun (and/or Peruru/Soleil) evaluates the threat
2. **Deployment**: Unseals appropriate Cardbot(s) from Metal Card(s)
3. **Weapon Activation**: Slots the weapon Metal Card into Metal Breath
4. **Combat Phase**: Strategic exchanges, often requiring multiple Cardbots
5. **Ultimate Technique**: Finishes the fight
6. **New in S2**: Peruru can also seal/deploy using Mukara's Metal Breath, creating dual-commander tactics
7. **New in S3**: Wild Cardbots have Beast Virus payloads in certain weapons (Phantom Hornet's Venospear, Night Griffon's Crimson Lance), adding a biological warfare element. Grand Bluleon requires both Jun and Soleil to cooperate.

### Key Themes (Season 2)
- **Ancient bonds transcending time**: The 4,000-year-old friendship between Cardbots and Porcaca Village still matters
- **Loyalty vs. independence**: Red Blitz's loyalty to Gigantrex vs. accepting new leaders; Sky Gallop's love of freedom vs. his bond with Peruru
- **Acceptance**: Deep Bite's arc about being accepted despite being "different" (a Wild Cardbot among Metal Cardbots)
- **Manipulation vs. genuine leadership**: Flame Nova enslaving Cardbots vs. Jun earning their trust
- **Healing**: Both physical (Gigantrex's head wound) and emotional (Rock Crush's trauma)

### Key Themes (Season 3)
- **Coexistence across boundaries**: Metal Cardbots, Wild Cardbots, and humans must all learn to live together
- **Control vs. partnership**: Soleil forcing obedience vs. Jun's friendship model
- **Nature vs. civilization**: The Beast Virus as a metaphor for the wild, untamed aspects of identity
- **Loss and purpose**: Solidhorn losing his entire tribe; Blaion as sole survivor of his tribe
- **Unity as strength**: Grand Bluleon as the literal manifestation of cooperation between all factions
