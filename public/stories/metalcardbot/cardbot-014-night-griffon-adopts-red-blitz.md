# The Knight Who Adopted a Little Brother

## Night Griffon's Most Heroic Quest Yet (According to Him)

It started, like most disasters in Mowtown, with Night Griffon declaring something dramatically to no one in particular.

He was standing on the tallest rooftop of Edo Motors, one foot up on the ledge, his black-and-gold armor gleaming in the afternoon sun, his Crimson Lance planted beside him like a flagpole. A pigeon landed on his helmet. He didn't notice.

"A knight," he announced to the open sky, "is nothing without a noble cause."

The pigeon cooed.

"A knight," he continued, ignoring it, "must protect the weak. Defend the innocent. Guide the lost."

The pigeon pooped on his shoulder.

Night Griffon still didn't notice. He was having A Moment.

Down in the garage below, Red Blitz was having a completely different kind of moment — specifically, the kind where you're kicking a soda vending machine because it swallowed your coin.

"GIVE ME MY DRINK, YOU RECTANGULAR MENACE!" Red Blitz roared, delivering a Crimson Kick to the poor machine. The vending machine wobbled but held firm, probably out of sheer spite.

Night Griffon, still on his rooftop, heard the commotion and looked down. His green hexagonal eyes narrowed thoughtfully. There, below him, was a red Cardbot — small by his standards, young, clearly troubled, clearly alone, clearly in need of *guidance*.

A tear almost formed in Night Griffon's optic.

"A lost soul," he whispered. "A youth without a path. A little warrior crying out for a mentor."

Red Blitz was not, in fact, crying out for anything except a grape soda. But Night Griffon had already decided.

He leapt from the rooftop, wings catching the air, lance held high, and landed in the middle of the garage with a dramatic *THOOM* that cracked the concrete.

Red Blitz, mid-kick, froze. Then he slowly turned his head.

"...Who are you and why did you break the floor."

"YOUNG WARRIOR!" Night Griffon boomed, striking a noble pose. "I have seen your struggle! I have heard your cry! And I, Night Griffon, Knight of the Storm, have come to answer!"

"What."

"From this day forth," Night Griffon continued, placing a gauntleted hand on his own chest, "I shall be your elder brother! Your guardian! Your shining example of knightly virtue!"

"...WHAT."

"Fear not, little one!" Night Griffon swept his lance in a grand circle. "I shall teach you all I know! Together we shall walk the path of honor! You are no longer alone!"

Red Blitz stared at him for exactly three seconds. Then he pointed at the vending machine.

"I was kicking a vending machine."

"A valiant battle!"

"Because it ate my coin."

"A noble grievance!"

"I'm *not* your little brother."

"A humble denial! Truly the mark of a worthy apprentice!"

Red Blitz slowly looked around the garage, hoping someone — anyone — would explain what was happening. Cielo, passing by with a clipboard, made direct eye contact with him, smiled, and walked the other way as fast as possible. Musclehyde suddenly became fascinated by a lightbulb. Dexter actually jumped into a tool cabinet and closed the door.

They were ALL leaving him alone with this.

"I already have a father figure," Red Blitz said flatly. "His name is Gigantrex. You may have heard of him. He's big. He's wise. He RAISED ME."

"A grandfather, then!" Night Griffon declared. "A wise elder! All the more reason you need a big brother to stand beside you!"

"THAT IS NOT HOW FAMILIES WORK."

"I have decided it is."

Red Blitz opened his mouth. Closed it. Opened it again. Finally just walked away.

Night Griffon followed him.

---

Over the next three days, Night Griffon committed himself to his new knightly quest with the same intensity he used to bring to combat. Which is to say: a LOT of intensity. Way too much intensity.

On Monday morning, Red Blitz woke up to find a hand-written scroll on his chest that said *"GOOD MORNING, LITTLE BROTHER. I HAVE PREPARED A MOTIVATIONAL SPEECH FOR THE DAY AHEAD. MEET ME ON THE ROOF AT DAWN. — YOUR ELDER, NIGHT GRIFFON."*

Red Blitz crumpled the scroll and threw it out the window.

Five minutes later, Night Griffon flew past the window, caught the scroll in midair, and slid it back under the door with a note that said *"YOU DROPPED THIS."*

On Monday afternoon, Red Blitz tried to pick a fight with a street sign that had mildly offended him by existing. Before he could land a single kick, Night Griffon swooped down from the sky and planted his Knight Shield between them.

"A true warrior does not waste his fire on metal posts, young brother!"

"GET OUT OF MY WAY."

"I shall not! As your elder, I must protect you from such indignities!"

"IT'S A SIGN. IT SAYS 'NO PARKING.' I'M NOT EVEN A CAR RIGHT NOW."

"Your feelings are valid, little brother."

Red Blitz made a sound that was not quite a word. It was more of a scream made of pure feeling.

On Monday evening, Night Griffon prepared "a brotherly bonding meal." This involved the stove, three pots, a lot of olive oil, and absolutely zero cooking knowledge. Red Blitz came home to find the kitchen on fire and Night Griffon, in full armor, proudly presenting a blackened lump on a plate.

"I have cooked for you, little brother! It is the dish of my people — which I have just invented!"

"WHAT PEOPLE. YOU'RE A RECONNAISSANCE JET."

"The people of *valor*."

"THE KITCHEN IS ON FIRE."

"A minor detail."

Red Blitz put out the fire with a fire extinguisher, took one look at the blackened lump, and walked into the garden to scream into a bush.

---

By Tuesday, Red Blitz was plotting escape routes. Real ones. He'd drawn a map. He was considering faking his own sealing.

On Wednesday, everything fell apart — but not in the way Red Blitz expected.

He'd gone out alone, on foot, in vehicle mode, blowing off steam by cruising the back roads outside Mowtown. No mission. No big brother. No "brotherly bonding." Just him, the road, and the wind.

And then, on a quiet stretch by the ravine, his front wheel caught a pothole at 140 kilometers per hour.

The Toyota 86 frame went airborne. Red Blitz transformed mid-flight out of pure instinct, hit the dirt hard, rolled, and came to a stop at the edge of a steep drop with his left leg twisted wrong and his Boost Gambiera cracked down the side.

He tried to stand. Couldn't.

He tried his comm. Out of range.

He tried to call out. No one was there.

For the first time in a long time, Red Blitz — the Red Flash, fight-happy terror of the Speranza crew — felt genuinely, quietly alone. He sat on the dirt with his damaged leg stretched out in front of him and stared at the sky going orange with sunset. He thought about Gigantrex, far away, probably busy. He thought about Cielo and Musclehyde, probably laughing at something back home. He thought about how stubborn he'd been all day.

And then he heard a sound like a jet engine.

A shadow passed over the ravine.

Night Griffon descended from the sky like a gold-trimmed thunderbolt, wings folded tight, lance strapped across his back, and landed in a cloud of dust right next to Red Blitz.

He didn't strike a pose. He didn't make a speech. He didn't even call him "little brother."

He just knelt down, looked at Red Blitz's twisted leg, and said: "Where does it hurt?"

Red Blitz blinked.

"...How did you even find me?"

"I was following you at a respectful distance." Night Griffon paused. "I have been following you at a respectful distance all week. I was afraid you would do something reckless."

"I was very reckless."

"I am aware."

There was a long quiet moment. Night Griffon carefully lifted Red Blitz's damaged leg, checked the cracked Gambiera, and tore a panel from his own gold shoulder plating to brace it. He did it gently. More gently than anyone would have believed Night Griffon capable of.

"Why do you keep doing this?" Red Blitz finally asked. His voice was smaller than usual. "Why me? You don't even *know* me."

Night Griffon was silent for a while.

"When I was a Wild Cardbot," he said eventually, "I had no one. I fought everyone I met. I thought fighting made me great. It didn't. It just made me lonely." He tightened the brace on Red Blitz's leg. "When I was sealed and woke up on Jun's side, I watched how your crew treated each other. Gigantrex watching over you all. Cielo teasing Musclehyde. You, kicking things when you're upset, and nobody holding it against you."

He looked at Red Blitz.

"I have never had a family. I do not know how to be in one. But I saw you alone at that vending machine, and I thought — maybe I could learn. Maybe I could be the kind of brother I would have wanted, once."

Red Blitz didn't say anything for a long time.

Then, quietly: "The vending machine stole my coin."

"I know."

"And the street sign was rude."

"I'm sure it was."

"And the food you cooked was the worst thing I've ever seen in my entire life."

"Also true."

Red Blitz almost laughed. Almost. His shoulders shook just a little.

"You're really bad at this big brother thing," he said.

"I am aware."

"Like, *really* bad."

"I shall accept your critique with the humility of a true knight."

Red Blitz did laugh then, a short surprised laugh that caught him off guard. Night Griffon's green eyes brightened.

---

By the time Night Griffon carried him back to the garage — walking, not flying, because he said "a brother carries his brother on solid ground" — the sun had fully set and the Speranza crew was in full panic mode.

Gigantrex met them at the door. His massive frame filled the whole entryway. He took one look at Red Blitz's braced leg, one look at Night Griffon still wearing his broken shoulder plate, and said nothing at all for a long moment.

Then he put a huge hand on Night Griffon's good shoulder.

"Thank you," Gigantrex said quietly. "For bringing him home."

Night Griffon straightened so fast he almost dropped Red Blitz.

"It was my honor, sir! As his elder brother, it was my sworn duty to —"

"Night Griffon."

"Yes, sir?"

"You do not have to make a speech every time."

Night Griffon considered this deeply.

"I will try, sir. I cannot promise."

Gigantrex almost smiled. "Welcome to the family."

---

Later that night, Red Blitz sat on the garage roof with his leg propped up and a grape soda in his hand (Night Griffon had gone back and fought the vending machine personally; it had surrendered immediately). Night Griffon sat beside him, polishing his Crimson Lance and humming an old Machina battle anthem off-key.

"Night Griffon."

"Yes, little brother?"

"Don't call me that in front of Cielo. He'll never let me live it down."

"Understood."

A pause.

"And don't cook again."

"Understood."

A longer pause.

"...But the motivational scroll was kind of nice."

Night Griffon's shoulders puffed up about three inches. He tried very hard not to smile. He did not succeed.

"I shall prepare another for tomorrow morning."

"Make it a short one."

"I shall *try*, little brother. I cannot promise."

Red Blitz rolled his eyes, but he was smiling too. Just a little. Just enough.

Above them, the Mowtown sky was full of stars, and somewhere in the garage below, Cielo whispered to Musclehyde, "Did Red Blitz just *giggle*?" and Musclehyde whispered back, "I think our family just got bigger."

---

## The Lesson

Family isn't always the one you're born into. Sometimes it's the one that picks you up when you fall, shows up when you're alone, and keeps trying even when you push them away. It can look ridiculous. It can involve burnt food, dramatic speeches, and pigeons on helmets. But if someone keeps choosing to be there for you — even badly, even loudly, even while tripping over their own lance — that's love. And love, when you let it in, always finds a way to fit.
