# Speranza Showdown: The Cosmic Stone Heist

## When Ancient Power Meets Modern Chaos

The alarm at Edo Motors went off at precisely 2:47 AM on a Tuesday.

This was not ideal timing. Red Blitz was in the middle of an argument with Jun about whether muscle cars were superior to police cars (they were, obviously, and Red Blitz was winning). Musclehyde had been mid-interview with a bewildered Teo about "what humans dream about" for his ongoing Earth documentary. And Rock Crush had *finally* — *finally* — fallen asleep after three hours of Glober's bedtime stories about Patagonia.

"INTRUDER ALERT," Edo's voice crackled through every speaker in the building. "VAULT BREACH DETECTED. SECTOR 7 — THE COSMIC STONE SAFE."

There was a beat of silence.

Then Red Blitz was already on his feet, golden crest blazing. "COSMIC STONES?!"

---

### Episode 1: Three-Pronged Assault

Jun Grant burst into the surveillance room, Metal Breath clutched in his fist, Peruru one step behind him with her Machinasium spear already uncased. On the monitors, three red signals blinked across the compound map like furious warning lights.

"Three teams," Peruru said, her sharp red eyes scanning the display. Her twin braids swayed as she leaned in. "North perimeter — ground assault. South bay — the water access channel. East wall — aerial approach."

"The Stone Raiders," Edo muttered, knuckles white on his tablet. "We picked up chatter about them last week. Rogue Cardbots who hunt for power sources. They must have traced the Cosmic Stones from Flame Nova's last known location."

Jun's jaw set. If the Stone Raiders got those Cosmic Stones, they could corrupt every Cardbot in Mowtown in minutes. He'd seen what a single Cosmic Stone did to Flash Vector and Spark Bit. Three vaults full would be catastrophic.

"Peruru," Jun said.

She was already nodding. "You take south and east. I'll take north."

"Who said the human gets to give orders?" Red Blitz crossed his arms, green eyes narrowed.

"I did," said Gigantrex.

The room went quiet. The massive red captain stood in the doorway, his Rex Arm gauntlet fully deployed, his light-blue eyes cutting through the darkness. Even with his head wound long since repaired by Peruru's spear, he carried an authority that made the air feel heavier.

Red Blitz coughed. "*...Fine.*"

---

### Episode 2: Deep Waters, Dark Tunnels

**South Bay — Jun Grant's Team**

The Stone Raiders' aquatic assault team hit the water access point at 2:52 AM. Three blocky submarine-form Cardbots tore through the bay, armed with violet-streaking energy cannons that lit the dark water like lightning in a bottle.

Jun raised his Metal Breath.

**"DEEP BITE — DEPLOY!"**

The card slot snapped open with a flash of gold. From the shimmer of energy, Deep Bite erupted from the bay itself in vehicle mode — his shark-head submarine cutting through the dark water with the grace of something born there. In one fluid motion he converted to robot form, landed on the dock in a crouch, yellow eyes glowing, Deep Trident already in hand.

The Stone Raider leader — a barrel-chested brute named Tonnage — aimed his cannon. "You can't stop all three of us with one fish-bot."

Deep Bite's snorkel-fused jaw shifted into something that might have been a smile. "I don't need to stop all three of you." He leveled the trident. "Just the front one."

**"TRIDENT CANNON!"**

The trident blade launched like a harpoon, pierced the first Raider's engine block clean through, and the bot crumpled with a screech of shredding metal. Before the other two could react, Deep Bite had already converted back to submarine mode, circling under the second Raider's legs like a ghost.

"He's *fast!*" Tonnage snarled.

"He's the Outlaw of the Sea," Jun said from the dock, grinning. "He's been fast since before your crew was built."

The **Trident Gatling** spun up with a sound like a thousand rain drops, and the water turned white with pressure bolts.

---

**Underground — Peruru's Team**

Peruru took the north perimeter. What she didn't expect was a tunnel-bore — a modified drill-unit Raider eating through the foundation toward the vault from below, vibrating the entire floor with a grinding scream.

She could feel it through the soles of her sandals.

"Rock Crush!"

The indigo Cardbot hesitated exactly half a second — flashback to the electrocution, to the noise, to the overwhelming chaos that had once shattered him. His crimson drill symbols caught the emergency lighting.

*I won't run away anymore.*

"I've got it!" Rock Crush's Megalo Drill gauntlets hummed to life with freezing blue energy. He dropped straight through the corridor floor — drilling through it with both arms spinning — and disappeared.

Below, the bore-bot looked up.

Rock Crush stared down from the freshly drilled hole.

"Rock Crush," Rock Crush announced, "is *HERE!*"

He came down like a meteor, both Megalo Drill halves unified on one arm and spinning at maximum speed. The **Spiral Iceberg** technique met the bore-bot's drill mechanism and instantly crystallized it solid — gears, pistons, the entire rotating assembly locked in a column of blue-white ice.

Peruru dropped through the hole behind him. "Nice entrance."

"I've been practicing," Rock Crush admitted, a little shyly. Then he noticed the ice was slowly spreading up the bore-bot's legs and added, "...Maybe a little *too* much."

---

### Episode 3: The Sky War

**East Wall — Aerial Assault**

The third Stone Raider team was airborne — six flying units screaming in from the east with drop-deployers designed to punch through the vault skylight from above.

Peruru activated Mukara's Metal Breath. "Cielo — *now!*"

The cerulean Cardbot unfolded from the flash of energy and hit the night sky like a thunderbolt, shoulder wings igniting his boosters with a roar. **"He who conquers the sky — it is I, Cielo!"**

The Stone Raiders opened fire immediately. Cielo didn't dodge. He *punched* through the first volley and fired back — both Nitro Express machine guns tearing arrow-shaped energy bullets through the formation so fast the Raiders barely had time to break apart.

"There are six of them!" Peruru called from the rooftop below.

"I know!" Cielo shot back, banking hard, his green eyes bright with focus. "Are you keeping count, or are you worried?"

"I'm *calculating!*"

"Calculating is just worrying with math!"

"CIELO!"

He grinned and reconfigured both arms into missile launchers. The **Homing Magnum** rounds spiraled out — guided, precise, relentless — and took down three Raiders before the remaining three finally broke formation in a panic, scattering to the wind.

Two more broke for the skylight from a different angle. Cielo folded his wings and dropped between them like a comet.

**Sky Jet Shot** at point-blank range.

The vault skylight remained perfectly intact. The two Raiders did not.

---

### Episode 4: The Indiana Jones Gambit

Inside the compound, Glober and his mannequin "Jones" had been supposed to hold a secondary checkpoint.

"Jones" was currently propped against a support pillar wearing Glober's safari hat and a sign that read *"DO NOT PASS — VERY DANGEROUS GUARD."*

Glober himself was hanging from the ceiling by his Shadow Whip, watching four confused Raiders circle the mannequin suspiciously.

He waited. Let them get closer. Let them really *commit* to being fooled by a drawing of a face on a fabric head.

Then — **"THUNDER WHIP!"**

The Shadow Whip cracked, binding all four Raiders in a single sweeping arc. The electric current hit a half second later and the Raiders went rigid, sparking, before collapsing in a twitching pile.

Glober swung down and landed next to Jones. He adjusted the mannequin's hat.

"Good work, partner," he said.

Jones stared back with his painted-on smile.

"Don't let it go to your head."

---

### Episode 5: Vault Defense — The Speranza Crew Stands Together

But the real threat was already inside.

Their commander — a hulking Raider named Seismar — had used the chaos above as cover, slipping into the compound through a maintenance shaft. He was already at the vault door, hydraulic claws prying it open, and three Cosmic Stones were already in his grip, pulsing with cold prism light.

"*Finally,*" Seismar breathed.

"Step away from the vault."

Gigantrex stood at the far end of the corridor. Alone, massive, Rex Arm filling the hallway. Behind him, Musclehyde flanked left — all four arms deployed, the Crane Bhuh crackling with green energy. Red Blitz flanked right — Boost Gambiera burning with crimson flames, his golden crest lit like fire. And from behind them came the smooth mechanical roll of Blastrain's wheels on the corridor floor, the train conductor Cardbot settling into position with absolute calm.

"Passengers," Blastrain announced pleasantly, "I'm afraid this station is now closed. Please surrender peacefully — the next stop will be *very unpleasant.*"

Musclehyde raised one of his four hands. "Before we begin — quick question for my documentary. Mr. Seismar, what drove you to a life of cosmic crime? Was it economic hardship, or a general disillusionment with—"

Seismar snarled and charged.

The Cosmic Stones crackled against his armor. His body had already started to go berserk, his eyes glowing with the corrupted energy, his movements wild and explosive.

He was bigger, stronger, and absolutely losing his mind.

He tore through Musclehyde's first **Brachio Slash** with sheer brute force. He powered through two of Red Blitz's **Crimson Kicks** before staggering, barely. He deflected Blastrain's **Crossover Storm** with his shoulder armor and kept coming.

But when Seismar raised his hydraulic claw for the killing blow on Red Blitz —

**"DEATH GRIP!"**

Gigantrex closed the Rex Arm's fangs around Seismar's entire torso.

The corridor *shook.* The lights flickered. Seismar thrashed with berserk strength — but the Death Grip's pressure was absolute, clamping tighter with every second, the T-rex fangs leaving dents in metal that would never fully straighten.

"You chose the wrong vault," Gigantrex said quietly, "and the wrong enemy."

He slammed Seismar into the floor.

Peruru was already sprinting in from the corridor entrance, Machinasium spear leveled like a lance. One thrust — directly into the Cosmic Stone cluster embedded in Seismar's arm. The prism shattered. The berserker energy drained out of Seismar in a single hollow breath, and he slumped, still, the fight completely gone.

Jun ran in behind her. "Is everyone okay?"

Red Blitz was already brushing off his shoulder armor like the fight had been a minor inconvenience. "Obviously. Did you doubt us?"

"I was handling my own battle!"

"And how did that go?"

"...Deep Bite sank two of them by himself."

Red Blitz's expression shifted to something almost — *almost* — impressed. "The fish is useful."

From somewhere down the corridor, Deep Bite's voice carried over the comms: "I heard that."

"I wasn't talking to you," Red Blitz said.

"I know. That's what made it nice."

Musclehyde was already pulling out his notepad. "Fascinating. Jun — quick interview — would you characterize this victory as primarily coordinated strategy, or individual excellence?"

"Musclehyde," Jun said, pinching the bridge of his nose, "we just stopped an armed robbery at three in the morning. *Can the interview wait?*"

"Reporters don't wait," Musclehyde said serenely. "News waits for no one."

### The Lesson

**True strength isn't about having the most power — it's about using the right strength at the right time.**

Every member of the team that night faced a completely different challenge. Deep Bite in the water. Rock Crush underground. Cielo in the sky. Glober in the corridor. The Speranza Crew at the vault.

None of them could have handled all of it alone.

When Seismar tried to use the Cosmic Stones, he thought raw power would be the answer. But power without partnership — like a Cosmic Stone without someone to trust — just turns you berserk. The Stone Raiders failed not because they were weak, but because they only knew how to fight *for* something, never *with* someone.

The Speranza Crew had been friends for 4,000 years — sealed in stone, separated by time, but the bond was still there the moment they stood together in that corridor.

**Remember:** The team you trust is stronger than any power-up that promises to make you unstoppable alone.
