# Wild Code: The Night the Jungle Woke

## When the Ground Itself Becomes Your Enemy

Nobody expected the construction crew to dig up a Machina artifact under Platform 9 of Mowtown Central Station.

To be fair, nobody expected the Machina artifact to then start *leaking.*

The first sign was the pigeons. They flew off the station roof at 6:15 AM in a single eruption of wings and panicked cooing, like someone had pulled a fire alarm only birds could hear. The second sign was the maintenance bot near the dig site, which slowly raised its head, opened its cargo claws like a flower blooming in fast-forward, and let out a sound that was completely wrong for a machine designed to move equipment crates.

It sounded like a growl.

By 6:22 AM, Mowtown Central Station was no longer a train station. It was a jungle.

---

### Episode 1: Two Teams, Zero Agreement

Soleil arrived at Platform 9 at 6:31 AM.

Jun Grant arrived at 6:32 AM. One minute late, hair still messy, half a breakfast sandwich in his hand.

Soleil looked at him. Then at the breakfast sandwich. Then back at him. Her amber eyes conveyed a volume of judgment that words couldn't contain.

"You're late," she said.

"I was *asleep,*" Jun said. "It's six in the morning."

"Threats don't follow a schedule."

"I know, but I do."

From the platform beyond the barrier gate, the sound of metal crashing against metal rang out, followed by a deep mechanical hiss. The entire building shook — a slow, rhythmic shudder, like a heartbeat, like breathing.

Jun and Soleil looked at each other.

"You sealed Blaion and Phantom Hornet, right?" Jun said.

"Yes," Soleil said.

"Good. Because I'm about to unseal everyone I have."

Soleil's expression didn't change — it rarely did — but she stepped through the gate beside him, her Wild Cardbot Metal Breath glowing faint orange at her wrist. A beat. A concession.

"Don't tell them what to do," she said. "Show them what needs doing."

Jun blinked. "That's... actually good advice."

"I know," said Soleil.

---

### Episode 2: The Jungle Under the Station

The Beast Virus artifact — a sealed waste canister from Planet Machina, buried under the station for unknown centuries — had cracked open along a fracture line thirty meters underground. The concentrated Beast Virus spores had already flooded the maintenance tunnels and were now rewriting every mechanical system they touched.

Escalators had grown fangs.

The ticket turnstiles were trying to eat commuters.

Three cargo-handling robots had fused together into something with six arms and no patience for authority, and it was currently attempting to knock down a support pillar just to watch the ceiling fall.

"Tigun," Jun said, raising his Metal Breath. "DEPLOY!"

The white-and-teal tiger-motif Cardbot landed on the platform with a *BOOM* that cracked the tiles, his Growl Cannonaxes already in hand. He looked at the six-armed cargo fusion, tilted his head, and then said — in the exact tone of someone deeply offended — "It's trying to take down a structural pillar. *That's* my move."

"Tigun, focus!"

"I am focused. I'm focusing on how unimpressive that thing is." He raised both axes. **"TIGER CANONADE!"**

The green energy beams hit the cargo fusion center-mass and punched it clean across the platform. It smashed into a closed kiosk and didn't get back up.

Tigun lowered the cannonaxes and flicked his tail fin. "See? Not impressive."

"There are about *forty more,*" Jun said.

A beat.

"...Okay," Tigun said. "That's more impressive."

---

### Episode 3: The Silent War in the Shadows

The Beast Virus didn't only infect large machines. The smaller infected units — maintenance drones, scan bots, vending robots — had gone completely feral and were swarming the lower tunnels by the hundreds, too fast and too small for heavy-hitters to catch efficiently.

This was exactly Phantom Hornet's kind of problem.

Soleil held up her Metal Breath. The hexagonal-patterned device glowed. "Phantom Hornet." A pause. Then — not a command, exactly. More like: "*Please.*"

The burgundy ninja Cardbot materialized from the shadow of a support column. He hadn't needed to be unsealed; he'd been watching for the last three minutes from concealment, cataloguing movement patterns. His green hexagonal eyes swept the tunnel once more.

Then he stepped forward without a word.

The **Phantom-Legion** activated — ten holographic copies of Phantom Hornet scattered through the tunnel in an instant, moving in every direction, drawing the feral drone swarm into the open. The real Phantom Hornet moved through the chaos like a ghost, his Buzz Sting's kunai-stinger spinning at drill speed, deactivating infected units with surgical precision rather than brute force.

**Sting Raid** — three fencing strikes that disabled the neural link in each drone without destroying the hardware.

**Venospear** — not fired offensively, but used as a localized pulse to overload the Beast Virus rewrite signal in a six-meter radius.

In two minutes, the tunnel was clear.

Phantom Hornet stood still in the center of it, propellers folded.

Soleil had followed behind him, watching. "You didn't need orders," she said.

"I rarely do," Phantom Hornet said. His voice was quiet, careful. Like everything he did. "A ninja reads the situation. Then acts."

Soleil was quiet for a moment. "Jun says something similar. He just usually trips over something first."

"...I know. I've watched him."

---

### Episode 4: The Night Griffon Problem

The real complication arrived at 7:04 AM.

The Beast Virus canister, as it turned out, wasn't just leaking fumes. It was *calling.* The concentrated spore cloud from the artifact was actively drawing infected machines toward itself, pulling them together into a larger collective entity — a growing amalgamation of corrupted metal that was absorbing every infected unit that reached it, growing bigger and angrier and considerably less reasonable by the minute.

It had now absorbed the ticket gates, the escalators, two vending machines, several baggage carts, and the station's emergency generator, making it roughly the size of a small building.

Night Griffon landed beside Jun with a *CLANK* that rattled the tiles, his gold-rimmed black armor catching the emergency lighting, his griffon-crest head tilted with absolute disdain.

"That," Night Griffon said, "is enormous."

"Good observation," Jun said. "Can you—"

"I will fight it," Night Griffon said immediately. "I, Night Griffon, will fight this amalgamation and prove once and for all that my combat ability is—"

"Night Griffon," Blue Cop W said from Jun's other side — his wolf-motif Season 3 form standing tall, the Howl Gauntlet wolf-cannon aimed at the amalgamation — "if you charge it headfirst, it will absorb you."

Night Griffon opened his mouth. Closed it. Opened it again.

"I was going to do a *strategic* charge," he said.

"You were going to do a *narcissistic* charge."

"Those are different things."

"They have the same outcome."

Night Griffon went very quiet. This was the look of a Cardbot performing calculations and not liking the results. "...What do you suggest?"

"We drive it *up,*" Blue Cop W said. "Force it out of the station and into open space. Then we hit it from multiple angles simultaneously. It can't absorb what it can't catch."

Night Griffon was silent for three full seconds. Then: "I will drive it upward. Strategically."

"Appreciated."

---

### Episode 5: Full Speed, Full Power

Tachy arrived at 7:11 AM.

Nobody had called her. She had just *heard* something interesting happening and appeared, yellow-and-orange cheetah armor blazing, Sonic Runner leg extensions already deployed, Cheetah Knuckles formed from her vehicle's front end and gleaming on both fists.

"What did I miss?" she said.

"Everything," Tigun said. "Also nothing. Also there's a giant virus-amalgamation about to come up through the floor."

"*Perfect,*" said Tachy, and she grinned.

The plan came together fast:

- Tigun: Ground level suppression. Keep it from spreading laterally.
- Night Griffon: **Storm Runner** centaur form, Scream of Talon attacks to herd it upward. 
- Phantom Hornet: Aerial flank from above with **Vernier** cannons, disable its outer shell.
- Tachy: **Flash Dash** at maximum speed, break the canister at the center of the mass.
- Blue Cop W: Cover Tachy's approach and hold the amalgamation's attention.

"And us?" Jun asked.

Soleil was already looking at him. For the first time that morning, her expression held something that wasn't quite calculation. Something quieter. "Together," she said.

Jun raised his Metal Breath. Soleil raised hers.

"SUPERIOR UNITE!"

The two Metal Breaths connected — Jun's and Soleil's — and the energy that erupted from both combined in a column of blue and amber light that went straight up through the station ceiling.

**Grand Bluleon** descended.

Blue Cop W's upper body, Blaion's lower half, the tribal relic blazing on the chest — one right eye firing lightning, one left eye pulsing flame. The voices of both Cardbots overlapping like a chord.

The amalgamation had no frame of reference for what it was looking at.

**"ALPHA OMEGA BUSTER!"**

The combined electric beam and fire blast from both gauntlets hit the amalgamation dead center — the wolf and lion aura heads forming in brilliant blue-red before the discharge — and the entire corrupted mass erupted in a cascade of light. The Beast Virus signal shattered. Every infected unit in the station disconnected at once, dropping like puppets with cut strings.

The station was quiet.

Dust settled.

Tachy emerged from the wreckage with the cracked Machina canister held in both hands, its leak sealed by the impact. "Got it."

Night Griffon stood in his centaur form, wings spread, looking deeply satisfied. "My herding strategy worked perfectly."

"It did," Blue Cop W said.

Night Griffon turned. "Did you just *agree* with me?"

"You did your job."

"I did it *excellently.*"

"You did it *well.*"

"I — fine." Night Griffon folded his wings. "Well is acceptable."

---

### Episode 6: After the Jungle

Mowtown Central Station reopened at noon. The platform damage was minor — patched quickly by Musclehyde and his crane. The infected bots were all recoverable once separated from the Beast Virus signal. The pigeons came back at 12:47 PM.

Soleil sat on the edge of Platform 9, watching Jun argue cheerfully with Tigun about whether tigersare stronger than lions (Tigun's position: yes, obviously, and also he was stronger than both). Blaion stood nearby, silent, his tribal relic catching the afternoon light.

"You didn't force any of them," Soleil said, to no one in particular.

"Hmm?" Jun turned.

"Your Cardbots. You didn't force them to cooperate with mine. You just... put them in the same situation and let them figure it out."

Jun shrugged. "That's kind of how team-ups work. You can't make two people trust each other. You can only give them a reason to."

Soleil was quiet. Her amber eyes tracked Night Griffon, who was now engaged in what looked like a heated debate with Phantom Hornet about stealth versus presence (Night Griffon's position: presence was more impressive; Phantom Hornet's position, delivered in three quiet sentences, was completely demolishing it).

"My mother sent me to collect Wild Cardbots," Soleil said finally. "For the vaccine. For their safety. She never told me..." she paused, "...that they already knew how to be safe. That they just needed somewhere to *be.*"

Blaion placed one hand gently on her shoulder. His hexagonal eyes were warm.

Jun smiled. "Well. Now they have somewhere to be."

---

### The Lesson

**You cannot force trust. You can only build it — together.**

Soleil started the day believing that giving clear orders and enforcing them was the best way to protect Wild Cardbots. She wasn't wrong to care. But she learned something watching Phantom Hornet move through that tunnel on his own terms, watching Night Griffon accept feedback from Blue Cop without being commanded — watching the entire team function not because they were told to, but because they *chose* to.

True partnership isn't about control. It's about giving everyone the space to bring their best, and trusting that they will.

Grand Bluleon isn't just a combined form. It's a promise. A Metal Cardbot, a Wild Cardbot, and two humans — choosing to work together despite being completely different.

**Remember:** The strongest teams aren't the ones where everyone obeys the loudest voice. They're the ones where everyone trusts the person standing next to them.
